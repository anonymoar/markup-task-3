By HEDRICK SMITH

Talks continued yesterday in an attempt to avert a strike threatened for tomorrow by the union representing 1,050 clerical workers at Columbia University.

The union, District 65 of the United Auto Workers, was certified early this year after a long struggle as the workers’ bargaining representative. Since talks began two months ago, the union has sought a 10.5 percent annual ncrease. The university is offering 4.5 percent.

Yesterday’s negotiations broke up at 9:30 P.M. and talks were expected to resume today. The union has said picket lines will go up at 7:30 A.M. tomorrow if there is no agreement.
